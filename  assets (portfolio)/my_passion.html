My passion for protecting our environment started very early for me. I have always spent time exploring outside. Growing in Michigan allowed me to have access to all seasons, and plenty of outdoor areas to enjoy. As an adult now I go hiking, camping, swimming, etc. Being able to be out in nature is such a special privilege that many take for granted. When I had to start thinking about careers I wanted to be a marine biologist. This might have been influenced by the movie Finding Nemo and my slight obsession with nudibranchs (sea slugs). 

Now my path has shifted slightly away from the marine aspect, but my passion is still there. I recently moved to North Carolina and had an opportunity to participate in a beach clean up. It may be silly to others but this experience was always on my bucket list. When I was in high school I discovered I enjoy not only the environment, but for helping people too. I would bake cookies for our local homeless shelter. Baking sweets helped me decompress after a long day of school and I got to help someone in need. I feel this experience sparked something in me, I wanted a career that not only helped animals but people too.


This is a photo of me when I took a fieldtrip to a nature sanctuary. This trip unlocked something in me. I got the opportunity to sit by myself in the woods and observe what I saw all around me. It was an experience I would not trade for the world.
